===============================
MEAN SQUARED ERROR (MSE)
===============================

1. What is MSE?
--------------
MSE stands for:

Mean Squared Error

MSE is an evaluation metric used
to measure model quality.

--------------------------------

2. Purpose of MSE
--------------
MSE measures how far
predictions are from
actual values.

--------------------------------

3. How MSE Works
--------------
Step 1:
Calculate prediction errors.

Step 2:
Square each error.

Step 3:
Calculate average of squared errors.

--------------------------------

4. Why Square Errors?
--------------
Squaring errors makes
large mistakes much more significant.

Large prediction errors
receive heavier penalties.

--------------------------------

5. Important Understanding
--------------
MSE does not treat all
errors equally.

Bigger errors contribute
much more to the final score.

--------------------------------

6. Example
--------------
Errors:

10
10
100

MAE:

(10 + 10 + 100) / 3
= 40

MSE:

(10² + 10² + 100²) / 3

= (100 + 100 + 10000) / 3

= 3400

--------------------------------

7. MAE vs MSE
--------------
MAE:
Average prediction error.

MSE:
Average squared prediction error.

--------------------------------

8. Difference Between MAE and MSE
--------------
MAE treats errors linearly.

MSE penalizes large errors
more heavily.

--------------------------------

9. When MSE Is Useful
--------------
MSE is useful when
large prediction mistakes
are considered costly.

--------------------------------

10. Important Observation
--------------
A model may have:

Small MAE

but

Large MSE

if a few predictions
contain very large errors.

--------------------------------

11. Theory Reinforcement
--------------
Q: What is MSE?

A:
MSE is an evaluation metric
that measures average squared error.

--------------------------------

Q: Why do we square errors?

A:
To penalize large mistakes
more heavily.

--------------------------------

Q: Difference between MAE and MSE?

A:
MAE averages errors.

MSE squares errors first
and then averages them.

--------------------------------

Q: Which metric punishes
large mistakes more?

A:
MSE.

--------------------------------

12. Practical Reinforcement
--------------
Q: What happened after changing:

70000 → 100000

and evaluating again?

A:
Prediction error increased.

As a result:

MSE increased significantly.

--------------------------------

Q: Why did MSE react strongly?

A:
Because squared errors
grow rapidly for large mistakes.

--------------------------------

13. Important Learning Principle
--------------
MAE gives average error.

MSE gives average squared error.

MSE focuses more on
large prediction mistakes.
