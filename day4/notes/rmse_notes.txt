===============================
ROOT MEAN SQUARED ERROR (RMSE)
===============================

1. What is RMSE?
--------------
RMSE stands for:

Root Mean Squared Error

RMSE is an evaluation metric
built from MSE.

--------------------------------

2. RMSE Formula Understanding
--------------
RMSE means:

Square root of MSE

RMSE = √MSE

--------------------------------

3. Purpose of RMSE
--------------
RMSE brings squared error
back to the original unit
of the target variable.

--------------------------------

4. Salary Example
--------------
If target is salary,
RMSE is also interpreted
in salary units.

Example:

RMSE = 2000

means model error is roughly
₹2000.

--------------------------------

5. Why RMSE Needed?
--------------
MSE values can become very large
because errors are squared.

RMSE makes the value easier
to interpret.

--------------------------------

6. MSE vs RMSE
--------------
MSE:
average squared error.

RMSE:
square root of MSE.

--------------------------------

7. MAE vs RMSE
--------------
MAE:
simple average prediction error.

RMSE:
also shows prediction error
but gives more importance
to large mistakes.

--------------------------------

8. Important Understanding
--------------
RMSE does NOT bring MSE
back to MAE.

RMSE brings squared error
back to original target unit.

--------------------------------

9. Business Interpretation
--------------
RMSE is easier than MSE
for business users.

Example:

MSE = 4000000
is hard to explain.

RMSE = 2000
is easier to understand.

--------------------------------

10. When RMSE Is Useful?
--------------
RMSE is useful when:
large prediction mistakes
should matter more.

--------------------------------

11. Theory Reinforcement
--------------
Q: What is RMSE?

A:
RMSE means Root Mean Squared Error.

It is the square root of MSE.

--------------------------------

Q: Why do we calculate square root of MSE?

A:
To bring squared error back
to the original unit and make
interpretation easier.

--------------------------------

Q: Difference between MSE and RMSE?

A:
MSE:
average squared error.

RMSE:
square root of MSE.

--------------------------------

Q: Which metric is easier to interpret
for business users?

A:
RMSE is easier than MSE
because it is in the original unit.

--------------------------------

Q: Is RMSE same as MAE?

A:
No.

MAE is average absolute error.

RMSE is square root of
average squared error.

--------------------------------

12. Practical Reinforcement
--------------
Q: What happened after changing:

70000 → 100000

and retraining model?

A:
Prediction errors changed.

MSE and RMSE also changed.

--------------------------------

Q: Why does RMSE change when
large prediction errors exist?

A:
Because RMSE is based on MSE,
and MSE gives more weight
to large errors.

--------------------------------

Q: Why print MAE, MSE and RMSE together?

A:
To compare different ways
of measuring prediction error.

--------------------------------

13. Important Learning Principle
--------------
MAE:
easy average mistake.

MSE:
punishes large mistakes heavily.

RMSE:
punishes large mistakes
and is easier to interpret
than MSE.
