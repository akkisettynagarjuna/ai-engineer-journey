===============================
MAE vs MSE vs RMSE
===============================

1. Why Compare Metrics?
--------------
Different evaluation metrics
measure prediction errors
in different ways.

Each metric provides
a different perspective
about model performance.

--------------------------------

2. Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
--------------
MAE stands for:

Mean Absolute Error

MAE calculates the average
absolute prediction error.

Easy to understand.

--------------------------------

3. Mean Squared Error (MSE)
--------------
MSE stands for:

Mean Squared Error

MSE squares every prediction error
before calculating the average.

Large mistakes receive
much heavier penalties.

--------------------------------

4. Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)
--------------
RMSE stands for:

Root Mean Squared Error

RMSE is the square root of MSE.

It brings the error back
to the original target unit.

--------------------------------

5. MAE vs MSE
--------------
MAE:
Treats all errors equally.

MSE:
Penalizes large errors
much more heavily.

--------------------------------

6. MSE vs RMSE
--------------
MSE:
Average squared error.

RMSE:
Square root of MSE.

RMSE is easier to interpret
because it is in the original unit.

--------------------------------

7. MAE vs RMSE
--------------
MAE:
Easy to explain.

RMSE:
Highlights large mistakes
while remaining easy to interpret.

--------------------------------

8. Which Metric Should We Use?
--------------
There is no single best metric.

Choose based on
business requirements.

If average error matters:
MAE is useful.

If very large mistakes
must be penalized:
MSE or RMSE is preferred.

--------------------------------

9. Important Observation
--------------
Changing one prediction
from:

70000

to

300000

caused MSE to increase
much more than MAE.

Reason:
MSE squares large errors.

--------------------------------

10. Theory Reinforcement
--------------
Q: What does MAE measure?

A:
Average absolute prediction error.

--------------------------------

Q: What does MSE measure?

A:
Average squared prediction error.

--------------------------------

Q: Why does MSE punish
large mistakes more?

A:
Because squaring makes
large errors grow much faster
than small errors.

--------------------------------

Q: What is RMSE?

A:
RMSE is the square root of MSE.
It expresses the prediction error
in the original target unit.

--------------------------------

Q: Which metric is easiest
to explain to business users?

A:
Usually MAE because it directly
represents the average prediction error.

RMSE is also commonly used
when large mistakes are important.

--------------------------------

11. Practical Reinforcement
--------------
Q: Which metric increased
the most after changing
70000 to 300000?

A:
MSE.

Because squaring makes
large errors much larger.

--------------------------------

Q: Why compare MAE,
MSE and RMSE together?

A:
To understand different ways
of measuring prediction quality.

--------------------------------

12. Important Learning Principle
--------------
Do not memorize evaluation metrics.

Understand:
- what each metric measures
- when each metric is useful
- why different metrics
may lead to different conclusions.
